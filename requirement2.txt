### Cost Optimization and Scalability Analysis

The proposed architecture uses Deepgram Nova-3 for Speech-to-Text, GPT-4o mini for language understanding and response generation, and Google Neural2 for Text-to-Speech synthesis. 
This approach follows a pay-as-you-go pricing model and is highly suitable for low- to medium-scale deployments because it eliminates the need for managing dedicated infrastructure.
For workloads below approximately 10,000 conversations per month, managed cloud APIs are generally more cost-effective and operationally simpler.
Organizations only pay for actual usage while benefiting from automatic scaling, high availability, and reduced maintenance overhead.

As user traffic grows significantly beyond 10,000 conversations per month,
the cumulative API costs may become substantial. At that scale, self-hosted solutions such as Whisper for Speech-to-Text and Piper for Text-to-Speech, deployed on cloud platforms such as AWS or Azure, can become economically attractive. 
Although self-hosting introduces additional responsibilities including GPU provisioning, infrastructure management, monitoring, scaling, and maintenance, the per-conversation cost can decrease when serving large volumes of requests.
Therefore, the managed API architecture is recommended for MVPs, prototypes, and moderate-scale production deployments, 
while self-hosted alternatives should be considered for high-volume deployments where infrastructure costs can be distributed across a large number of users.
